This website

Independent project
2025-2026, Amsterdam

Ever since I was in high-school I wanted to create my own website, HTML being the first encounter with computer code I have had. Throughout the years, as the internet evolved, so did the tools for creating web content. With templates, libraries, free and proprietary software for website creation becoming increasingly sophisticated, leading up to the age of AI where digital content can be generated with a simple prompt, I was at a loss to shape an idea that would be a true and direct expression of myself and my knowledge.

My understanding of a website as a merging point for all my creative efforts aligned well with the reasons for the foundation of TheKnowDough. For this reason, I took the difficult path of writing it from scratch, using just a code editor and making a conscious choice about every little element in it. Despite my original motivation to code as means to automate processes, when faced with a possible future where coders are not considered necessary anymore, I wanted to embody the idea of digital craftsmanship in the project that presents my work and tells my story.

There was also an effort to represent the notion of constant evolution in the website. Each loading of a page triggers a unique animation of the logo, never to be repeated in the exact same way.

Seeing the current toll of technology on the climate, the environment and our social fabric, I am also becoming increasingly interested in concepts like permacomputing and the Small Web. This prompted me to try reducing my code to the bare minimum necessary and achievable with my knowledge that would accomplish the goals of the website. No external libraries were used, except Hydra for the display of my sketches created there. No bloatware, no tools for the tools for the tools for the tools...

This website also needs no cookies. I am not interested in gathering any of your personal data. I am not interested in locking your attention or selling you ads. If you want to contact me, I present you with other channels to do so. Perhaps, in time, this place could evolve to become a digital garden - still personal, still human, unpolished and ever-evolving. For now, it is simply meant to tell you my story.

Isn't this nice?
