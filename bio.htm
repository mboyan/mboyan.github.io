TheKnowDough was founded in Amsterdam in 2023 by me, Boyan Mihaylov. It weaves together previous paths of research and creation into a shape-shifting body of expertise.


I have always been interested in worldbuilding - the construction of speculative mirrors of our universe, shaped by coherent laws and relationships as the backdrop of intriguing stories.

My journey began in architecture - where worlds are built in the form of habitable space, reflecting social and existential necessities through the language of material and structure.

The revelation soon came that worlds can emerge algorithmically - fueled by stories told through mathematics. I discovered the common ground of design and programming during the early years of the Institute for Computational Design (ICD) at the University of Stuttgart, where biomimetics is commonly used as an inspiration tool for design and engineering. Through a close involvement in digitally fabricated medium-scale architecture projects, I entered the world of robotic and CNC fabrication and became familiar with mass customisation techniques.

It was during that time that I engaged with the Baubotanik Research Group, who envision a world in which human-made architecture and living plants symbiotically intertwine as biomechanical hybrids. For the first time I was confronted with the problem of modelling biological systems, in an attempt to construct simulation tools for the growth of living tree structures.

The technical computational design expertise could soon be shared during my role as a tutor at courses organised by DesignMorphine and CEDIM. So others could learn to shape their physical and digital worlds.

During my years as a computational design specialist at Joris Laarman Studio in Amsterdam, I refined the ability to interpret abstract conceptual ideas as precise geometric and algorithmic logic. This resulted in many fascinating projects, ranging from product design and small-scale architecture to augmented reality workflows.

In the meantime, my involvement in a DIY movement linked to the vibrant electronic music scene of Amsterdam led to various interactive installation projects, creative coding for party visuals and live-coding jams. My participation in organising electronic music events culminated in the co-founding of WP Frequencies, an international collective of DJs and visual artists. I have also been performing as a DJ under the alias fin.

I pursued my second degree in computational science at the University of Amsterdam. My understanding of algorithms as building speculative worlds found a new application. Delving into a plethora of scientific disciplines, all linked by the common platform of computation, I studies models of phenomena occurring in our own world. This included exercises in complex systems, self-organisation and pattern formation - molecular, biological and societal. For my graduation project, I explored the diffusion-based signalling mechanisms of mould spores by assessing different models of their interaction.

TheKnowDough marks my entry as an independent interdisciplinary professional, conceived as a shape-shifting entity that unites these paths of creation and research. Notable projects that have come to fruition under this endeavour are The Underground Tree in colaboration with Tanja Smeets and Diploria Lighting in colaboration with Michael Jasinski.
