TheKnowDough is an ever-evolving interdisciplinary practice that channels visions through algorithmic processes to solve, explore and produce manifestations of complexity.


Founded by Boyan Mihaylov in Amsterdam, TheKnowDough fuses ventures in product design, computational science, architecture, generative visuals and music.



These fields are united under common creative philosophy and ethics:

  • A focus on creating processes rather than static objects.
    Procedural design (creating instructions) enhances exploration compared to explicit crafting (following pre-defined instructions). A product is never in a finished state, but is subject to ongoing environmental changes.
  • Technology should aid curiosity, not stifle it.
    Computation tools are only valuable to human knowledge as long as they are an extension of human reasoning, inspire exploration and allow critical dissection.
  • Technology should aid a better world, not serve itself.
    Whether by advancing knowledge for mindful management of complex systems, or by subjecting itself to a responsible use of resources, computation should be an instrument for improving natural, not artificial, ecosystems.

    • Once men turned their thinking over to machines in the hope that this would set them free. But that only permitted other men with machines to enslave them.
    (Frank Herbert, Dune, 1965)


    Nature carries an inherent capacity to compute. The work of TheKnowDough is driven by a fascination with the natural intelligence found in biological, chemical and physical systems. This serves both as an inspiration (translating natural principles to form and function) and as an object of study (applying computational modelling to understand nature better).
