TheKnowDough is an ever-evolving interdisciplinary practice that channels visions through algorithmic processes to solve, explore and produce manifestations of complexity.


Founded by Boyan Mihaylov in Amsterdam, TheKnowDough fuses ventures in product design, computational science, architecture, generative visuals and music.



These fields are united under common creative philosophy and ethics:

  • A focus on creating processes rather than static objects.

    Our universe is dynamic.

    A product is never in a finished state, but is subject to ongoing environmental changes.

    Procedural design (creating instructions) enhances exploration compared to explicit crafting (creating objects).
  • Technology should aid, not stifle, curiosity.

    Computation tools are only valuable for human knowledge as long as they are an extension of human reasoning, inspire exploration and allow critical dissection.

    Black boxes and obfuscationism are counterproductive.
    • Once men turned their thinking over to machines in the hope that this would set them free. But that only permitted other men with machines to enslave them.

    (Frank Herbert, Dune, 1965)
  • Technology should serve a better world instead of itself.

    Whether by advancing knowledge for mindful management of complex systems, or by succumbing to a responsible use of resources, computation should ultimately improve natural ecosystems.



    • Nature carries an inherent capacity to compute.

    The work of TheKnowDough is driven by a fascination with the emergence found in biological, chemical and physical systems. This serves both as an inspiration - translating natural principles to algorithm, form and function - and as an object of study - using computational modelling to understand nature better.
